Colts History

The Indianapolis Colts began as the Baltimore Colts in 1953 and became one of the most successful teams in early professional football. Led by legendary quarterback Johnny Unitas, the team won NFL championships in 1958 and 1959, helping popularize the sport nationwide. In 1984, owner Robert Irsay controversially relocated the franchise from Baltimore to Indianapolis. The Colts reached new heights in the 2000s behind quarterback Peyton Manning, winning Super Bowl XLI after the 2006 season. Today, the Colts remain one of the NFL's most recognizable franchises, playing their home games at Lucas Oil Stadium in Indianapolis.
